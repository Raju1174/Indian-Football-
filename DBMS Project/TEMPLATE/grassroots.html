Grassroots

GRASSROOTS FOOTBALL

Grassroots Football lays the foundation of the sport, and its primary aim is to increase participation for children, youth, amateurs, veterans, those with physical disabilities, cerebral palsy, visually impaired and even the socially disadvantaged.

The AIFF Grassroots programme pays special attention to quality and quantity of licensed E-certificate coaches, accredited academies, grassroots festivals, Golden Baby Leagues, amateur football tournaments, futsal and anything which doesn't come under the umbrella of professional Football. AIFF is also determined to conduct numerous community outreach programs to popularise Football through grassroots.

The Asian Football Confederation (AFC) defines grassroots football as 'All football which is non-professional and non-elite.' It intends to bring all together by providing access to Football regardless of age, gender, physical condition, skin colour, religion or ethnic origin.

AIFF's Grassroots Development programme kicked-off in December 2012 and the expansion was rapid and fast. In 2013, AIFF finished second at the AFC Annual Awards in the 'Grassroots Member Association of the Year' category.

AIFF also bagged an AFC President Recognition Award for Grassroots Football in 2014. More recently, in February 2020, AIFF received the AFC Grassroots charter -- Bronze Level membership for its stupendous work towards promoting grassroots football.
